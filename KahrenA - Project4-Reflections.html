This project, compared to prior projects, required small amount of code yet it was interesting and challenging as it required me to adjust and tweak three parameters: Kp, Kd and Ki as the idea was to implement a PID controller.


The strategy I used was to start with a P-Controller and hone do a reasonable Kp value, then move to finding a reasonable Kd value and subsequently a Ki value.


It took several triesto conclude that my Kp value needed to be around 0.3. Any other value was oscillating and making the car swing left then right in an increasing manner and very quickly drove the car out of track… Kp of 0.3 seemed to last a bit longer before veering off track… I must say this was a surprise to me as I thought the amplitude of the gyration would be constant. The source of my impression were the lecture itself as well as the Udacity Artificial Intelligence for Robots course I took last year in preparation for this nanodegree. In looking at the material again, it is clear that there is no basis for my impression. The fig below even shows the P Controller graph is oscillating out.




Anyhow, next step, determining a reasonable Kd value. I knew that Kp needed to be about an order of magnitude smaller than Kp. This is what I had observed implementing Twiddle as part of the lecture.


Here I got side tracked by a bug then with the formula that we had used in the Robotics course which took into account the speed of the vehicle. D-term of the steering angle formula was :

d_term = Kd * d_error/speed;

Turns out we did not need to worry about the speed for this project.


Once beyond these issues, I experimented with Kd values around 0.03 and settled for 0.01. Given we are dealing with a closed feedback loop, CTE coming from the simulator, in response steering angle is returned which in turn calculates the next CTE, it became challenging to figure out why prior to going to a turn /curve the CTE values started to fluctuate.
